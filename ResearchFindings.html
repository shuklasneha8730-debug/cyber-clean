Abstract: Research Impact

Our study on cyber hygiene practices among college students and faculty reveals critical insights into digital behavior, risk awareness, and the need for targeted educational interventions.

01. Behavioral Analysis

Comprehensive survey of digital habits reveals a strong reliance on digital tools but inconsistent security practices among the academic community.

02. Risk Identification

Key vulnerabilities were identified in network security (Wi-Fi usage) and advanced threat response mechanisms, highlighting critical gaps.

03. Privacy Trends

While 80% manage social media privacy, only a fraction employ advanced tools like VPNs, indicating a "privacy illusion" rather than deep security.

04. Strategic Recommendations

Data supports the integration of mandatory cyber hygiene modules into the curriculum to bridge the gap between awareness and action.

Overall Behavior Profile

Analysis of cyber hygiene practices among college students and faculty highlights a mix of strong awareness and critical gaps.

✅

High Awareness

80% manage privacy settings carefully, showing strong basic security habits.

⚠️

Moderate Caution

55% verify links before clicking, but 40% still risk using public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks.

❌

Critical Gaps

Only 15% utilize VPNs or report threats, indicating a need for advanced training.

47% Overall Score

Key Insights & Conclusion

Deep dive into specific behaviors that define the current state of cyber hygiene on campus.

🎯

The Awareness Gap

While students are aware of basic risks, there is a significant disconnect between knowing best practices and implementing them consistently.

📶

Network Negligence

Public Wi-Fi is a major vulnerability. A large percentage of users perform sensitive tasks (banking, work) without VPN protection.

🔒

Password Fatigue

Users struggle with password management. Reusing passwords across multiple platforms remains a common issue despite known risks.
