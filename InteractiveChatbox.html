Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a password strong? +
A strong password uses a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols, and is at least 12 characters long.
How can I identify a phishing email? +
Look for urgent language, generic greetings, spelling errors, and suspicious sender addresses asking for personal information.
Is public Wi-Fi safe to use? +
Public Wi-Fi is often unsecured; avoid accessing sensitive accounts or use a VPN to encrypt your data.
Why are software updates important? +
Updates often contain security patches that fix vulnerabilities hackers can use to access your system.
What is Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)? +
2FA adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification, like a code sent to your phone.
How often should I back up my data? +
You should back up important files at least once a week to an external drive or secure cloud storage.
Is it safe to download free software? +
Only download from reputable sources; free software from unknown sites often contains hidden malware.
What should I do if my account is hacked? +
Immediately change your password, enable 2FA, and check for any unauthorized activity on your account.
How can I protect my privacy on social media? +
Set your profiles to private and avoid sharing personal details like your location, phone number, or birthday publicly.
What is a VPN and why do I need it? +
A Virtual Private Network encrypts your internet connection, protecting your data from snoopers on public networks.
