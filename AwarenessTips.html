Do's & Don'ts

Essential rules for maintaining digital security.

✅ DO's

  • Use complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Verify the sender's identity before clicking on links.
  • Keep your operating system and antivirus updated.
  • Use a secure VPN when on public Wi-Fi.
  • Regularly back up your important data to the cloud.
  • Lock your device every time you step away.

❌ DON'Ts

  • Share personal information on unsecured websites.
  • Use the same password for multiple accounts.
  • Ignore software update notifications.
  • Click on pop-up ads or suspicious attachments.
  • Leave your devices unlocked in public spaces.
  • Trust offers that seem "too good to be true".

Step-by-Step Guides

Follow these procedures to secure your digital life.

01

Setting Up 2FA

Double Your Security

Go to Account Settings > Security. Enable 2-Step Verification. Scan the QR code using Google Authenticator or SMS verification.

02

Creating Strong Passwords

Complexity is Key

Use at least 12 characters. Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Avoid dictionary words or personal dates.

03

Identifying Phishing

Stop, Look, Think

Check the sender's email for misspellings. Hover over links to see the real URL. Never download unexpected attachments.

Real Life Stories

Learn from real-world incidents.

Case Study Video: A conversation about how a student identified and reported a phishing email attempt.

The "Urgent" Email Scam

Scenario: Rahul received an email claiming his bank account would be frozen unless he clicked a link to verify his details immediately. The email looked official but had a slight spelling error.

Action: Instead of clicking, Rahul hovered over the link and saw it directed to a strange domain (.xyz instead of .com). He called his bank directly.

Outcome: The bank confirmed it was a phishing attempt. Rahul reported the email and saved his savings.
